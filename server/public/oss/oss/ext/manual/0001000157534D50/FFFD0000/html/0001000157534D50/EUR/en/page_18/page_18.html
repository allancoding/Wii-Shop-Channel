Tactics While Grappling

Certain moves are stronger against other moves while grappling.



Press > Throw (Lift)
A press allows you to keep a firm grasp and push the opponent down without the risk of being thrown or lifted up. It is good against opponents who use a lot of throws.

Throw > Parry
A throw allows you to release yourself from the opponent’s grasp by throwing him/her behind you and spinning around. You switch positions with the opponent, so it is also useful for turning the tables in a match.

Lift > Parry
Lifting up allows you to lift and carry your opponent forward. Although the effect is the same as a press, lifting is better against opponents who parry.

Parry > Press
A parry allows you to parry opponents who are attempting to move forward and release yourself from your opponent’s grasp. You switch positions with the opponent, so you can turn the tables in a match.

Grab (1 Button) and Break (2 Button)
A grab is a move where you attempt to firmly grasp the opponent’s mawashi. Raise the grab gauge to increase the accuracy of your throw, lift, and finishing move. A break, on the other hand, is a move that decreases the opponent’s grab gauge. The more you press grab and break, the more it’ll affect the grab gauge.

Tactics when Starting a Match

At the beginning of a match, the grab gauge will increase and decrease when the referee calls, "Ready!" Your Charge Power will be determined by the timing at which you press the 1 Button or the 2 Button. The higher the gauge, the better you'll charge your opponent when the match starts.

Other Tactics

The grab gauge level necessary for a throw differs depending on the opponent.
A successful throw depends not only on your Throw rating and grab gauge, but also the opponent’s weight, guts, etc. If you fail to do a throw when your grab gauge is at 5, try again when it is higher.

Use grabs to escape from a relentless strike attack!
There are two ways to escape from an onslaught of strikes at the edge of the ring:
1. Parry (Left on +Control Pad and 1 Button)
2. Grab (1 Button)

Although a parry allows you to instantaneously turn the tables on your opponent, it can be difficult to get the timing right. Therefore, you should probably try using a grab first. Grabbing works better when you and your opponent are not grappling, as you will not receive damage from strikes. In addition, you receive a “First Grab Bonus,” allowing you to launch a grappling attack with a stronger grab gauge level.
