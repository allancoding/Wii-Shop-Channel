Reneging
On any turn a player may choose not to play a card in hand, but he or she must then draw a card as though he or she had no play to make. If the card drawn is playable, the player may play that card (depending on the rules chosen for the match) but may not play a card already in his or her hand. Only the card drawn may be played.

Strategy of Reneging
Reneging can be useful at times. Imagine, for example, that your opponent has played his or her second-to-last card and it was a Wild card and he or she called it red. Most likely his or her last card is red. If you have a red card in your hand but you need a Wild card to change the color so your opponent will not go out, you can renege and draw a card, hoping that you will get a Wild card to change colors and thereby prevent your opponent from playing his or her last card.
