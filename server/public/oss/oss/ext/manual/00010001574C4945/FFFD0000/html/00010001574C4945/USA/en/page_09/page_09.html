Color Theory
In Liight, you will often have to blend two lights together to create the color you need to solve the puzzle. Before you can do this, you need to understand what colors make what!



Blending colored light is different than mixing colored paint. With light, the primary colors are red, green, and blue. Blending red and green makes yellow, blending green and blue makes cyan, and blending blue and red makes magenta. Red, green and blue together make white!

In some puzzles, you will see black x targets. These targets cannot have any light on them at all, or else the puzzle will not solve. You might have to use your light pieces to cast a shadow in order to keep all the light off of these targets.

The puzzle board itself can come into play, too. You will find you can't put lights on top of clear tiles or X Block tiles. X Block tiles will also block light and cast a shadow! You will quickly learn how to solve puzzles under all these constraints.

In later puzzles, you will encounter more complicated elements including Split Lights and Spinners. When you see a spinner on the board, place the cursor on its center and press the A button to rotate it and all the targets on it 90 degrees. You will have to discover which position each spinner must be set in as you solve the puzzle!
