Gameplay

The gameplay of LIT is based on the idea that lit areas are safe, and unlit areas are dangerous. If the player walks Jake into the darkness at any time, or allows darkness to fill an area where Jake is standing, he will be pulled into the shadows by creatures and that level will have to be replayed. Light is introduced by either turning on a light source directly, or firing a projectile at a light source in order to trigger it. When Jake stands close enough to use an object or collect an item, that object or item will glow red. At that moment, pressing the A button will use or collect it.

 

Most light sources in the game can be turned on and off. This is essential to gameplay, as most light objects fill the Light Meter, and must be used conservatively in order to pass through the more challenging levels. The exception is any windows, which (once shattered) cannot be re-sealed, and do not add to the Light Meter’s charge. Because Jake will not always be able to reach light objects that need to be turned off, electrical objects can also be destroyed from a distance with a slingshot or cherry bomb. Doing this reduces the Light Meter, however destroyed light sources can never again be used.

 

Each level begins with Jake at the entrance door, standing in that door’s limited square of light. From here, the player will always be able to reach a light source, or an item that can be used to affect a light source. Anytime a level is failed, the player will restart from this location.

 

In the following image, Jake is able to walk up a beam of light created from a broken window. However, a table blocks the player from continuing up the light path. Jake is standing next to a lamp, which glows red, indicating that it can be used by pressing the A Button.

 

 

By pressing the A Button, the lamp is turned on, creating enough light for the player to navigate around either side of the table and continue up the window’s beam.

 

 

Once the player has passed this obstacle, unless there is a reason to return to this area, this lamp may no longer be needed. The player can reduce the Light Meter by turning this object off once they’ve passed it.

 

 

When the Light Meter is filled to capacity, it will begin flickering on and off as a warning to the player that turning on any additional electrical lights will burst it. Each light object fills up the Light Meter by a specific amount. The following is based on the Light Meter having 100 units:

 

Window: None

Lamp: 20 units

PC Monitor: 10 units (per monitor)

Oscillating light: 20 units

Television: 25 units

Motion sensor: 30 units

 

In addition to being pulled into the shadows by creatures, Jake can also be knocked unconscious. This only occurs in boss levels, when a boss either strikes Jake or throws a projectile at him. When Jake is knocked unconscious, he will fall on the floor, and the player will restart that level. There is no health meter in the game, so getting struck just once will cause Jake to collapse.
