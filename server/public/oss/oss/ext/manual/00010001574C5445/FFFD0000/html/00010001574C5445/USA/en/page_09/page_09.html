Wii Remote

Pointing
Moves the game’s reticle, which allows the player to adjust where Jake is facing, aim while using the flare, slingshot, flashlight, or cherry bomb, and move the camera while in the aiming camera perspective.

 

Motion
Different motions and gestures made with the Wii Remote by the player allow Jake to recharge the flashlight, strike a flare, and throw a cherry bomb. On-screen instructions for these actions appear each time that action is required.

 

Speaker
The Wii Remote’s speaker can be held up to the ear to listen to phone calls made from Rachael to Jake.

 

A Button
Most actions in the game are triggered by pressing this button when Jake is either standing next to, or holding, a specific object. An icon at the top-right of the screen always displays what object will be used by pressing the A button at that moment. Using the A Button, the player can turn on electrical objects, collect an item, fire a slingshot pellet, lock aiming to throw a cherry bomb, prepare to strike a flare on the ground, answer a phone, and access the exit door. The A button is also used to confirm all selections in the Main Menu.

 

B Button
Pressing this button cycles through available items that have been collected in the current level. The B Button is also used to back out of most areas of the Main Menu.

 

+Control Pad left
Adjusts the in-game camera to a top-down perspective.

 

+Control Pad down
Adjusts the in-game camera to its default, tilted perspective.

 

+Control Pad right
Adjusts the in-game camera to a zoomed-in, tilted perspective.

 

+ Button
Pauses the game, and allows the player to continue, restart the current level, or exit to the Main Menu.

 

HOME Button
Pressing HOME will take the player to the HOME Menu, at which point the player will have the option of returning to the Wii Menu.
