Examples, Inventory & Energy

EXAMPLES


Let’s see some examples.
Door. If you point at a door with the Wii Remote and press the B Button, the icons of the magnifying glass (to look at the door) and the hand (to open the door) will appear in turn.
Bucket. If you point at a bucket, the icons of the magnifying glass will appear (to look what is inside of it) and the hand (to pick up the bucket).
Horse. If you point at  the horse, you can choose, by pressing the B Button, to ride it (with the hand) or to look at it (with the magnifying glass).
Girl. If you point at the girl, the icons of the magnifying glass will appear (to look at her) or the speech balloon (to talk to her).
Once you have chosen the icon that you want by pressing the B Button, you only have to press the A Button to make Fenimore do the action.

Examples:
If the cursor is on the horse, with the hand icon, and you press the A Button, Fenimore will ride it. If the cursor is on the girl, with the speech balloon icon, and you press the A Button, Fenimore will talk to her.

 

THE INVENTORY


When Fenimore picks up one object, it will go to the inventory, which is in the black band on the top of the screen.
By moving the cursor onto the inventory, you can choose different objects by pressing the A Button. The selected object will be stuck on the cursor. By moving the cursor, you can select the place of the scenario where you want to use the object.
In this way, you can give objects to other characters or use the inventory objects with others on the screen.


THE ENERGY


An energy bar will appear, on Fennimore’s horse when you move the cursor on it. If the bar is empty, it means that the horse must be fed in order to make him useful. The horse eats carrots, and one carrot fills a section of the energy bar. Depending on the distance made, the horse will spend more or less energy, but you can always find more carrots (cheap, abundant and nutritive food).
