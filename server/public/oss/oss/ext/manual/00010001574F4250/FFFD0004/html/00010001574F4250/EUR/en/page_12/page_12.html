12
Hints and Techniques

Keep this helpful information in mind and you’ll successfully navigate the numerous galaxies of Orbient in no time!

● Check Your Surroundings

You can look at your surroundings with while your star is a satellite. Look at the movement of stars around you to choose the right time to break out of orbit.

● Avoid Obstacles

You will encounter some objects that cannot be used to your advantage. It’s best to just avoid these obstacles. Asteroids do not have gravity (no G-lines) and are not affected by your gravity or antigravity pulls. You can’t make asteroids into satellites, and you can’t absorb them. Black holes have their own gravity, so if you get too close to one it will start to suck your star in. You will lose a life if you are sucked into the centre of a black hole.

● Try Hovering

If you press and quickly and repeatedly, you can do what’s called “hovering”. By hovering, you can make slight adjustments to your distance from stars or your drift direction.

● Don’t Get Too Large

You can absorb many stars in a stage. However, absorbing too many makes it easy to bump into things, and your star becomes less responsive to gravity and antigravity. As long as your star becomes as big as the goal star, you will be able to clear the stage, so be careful not to become larger than you need to.

● Making a Moon into a Satellite

When your star becomes as large as the goal star, a moon will appear somewhere in the stage. Be sure to look for it. If you make the moon a satellite and then clear the stage, you’ll get bonus points. The moon will disappear if you collide with it. If you clear all the stages with moons, something will happen!
