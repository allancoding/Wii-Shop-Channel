How to Play

Your star will already be moving when you start each stage. While you have no direct control over your star, you can influence its course through the skilful use of gravity and antigravity.

● Generating Gravity

Press to generate gravity. While is pressed, nearby stars will pull your star closer. When there are multiple stars nearby, how each star affects your star depends on the distance and size of the star.

Closer stars pull more strongly   Larger stars pull more strongly

● Generating Antigravity

Press to generate antigravity. While is pressed, nearby stars will repel your star.

Closer stars repel more strongly   Larger stars repel more strongly

● Absorbing Stars

You can safely collide with stars of equal size (blue stars) and stars smaller than yours (grey stars). Collide with a star of equal size to absorb it and become larger. Colliding with stars smaller than yours will absorb them but has no affect on your size, while colliding with stars larger than yours (red stars) causes you to lose a life.

When your star becomes larger, the colours of other stars on the stage will change to indicate their new status relative to your size. You will then be able to absorb stars that were previously too large but are now equal in size (red stars that have changed to blue stars).

Note: You cannot absorb asteroids or the goal star.

● Becoming a Satellite

When your star enters the red G-line around a star at a certain angle, it will become a satellite of that star.

When your star becomes the satellite of a star, it will continue to orbit it even if you don’t do anything. Your star will not collide with a star it’s orbiting.

If you want to break out of orbit, press to move away from the star.

Note: You cannot become a satellite of asteroids or the goal star.

● Making a Star Your Satellite

If a grey star crosses your star’s G-line at just the right angle, it will become a satellite.

When it does, the star’s G-line will disappear, and it will orbit your star. Clear a stage with satellites and you’ll get bonus points and increase your lives.

● Game Over

You have five lives when the game begins. You will lose a life if you collide with a star that is larger than yours, an asteroid or a gigantic planet. If you run out of lives, the game is over.
