Pro Points and Helpful Tips

Classic Lawn Darts – If a Classic game ends in a tie, Pro Points are used to determine a winner. This point system measures the distance of each dart from the center of the target, assigning a point value, and adding those points together.

501 – Since scoring as many points as possible is the ultimate goal, shoot for the highest number that you have consistency hitting. For many people this involves hitting the 20 or the triple 20. The only problem is the dangerous "1" just to the right of the 20. The 19 is another popular option, particularly if you can place it high near the bull's eye.

During the final tosses of a round, when it is possible for a player to "exit" the game on three successful combination tosses, a Double Out Hint chart appears on the left side of the screen. This chart reveals the exact combination of positions on the dartboard a player can hit to win the game. If a player hits a different number, the chart recalculates the outs based on the new number.

Cricket – Winning at Cricket requires controlling territory on the dartboard. Strategies vary depending on personal skill, but experts agree that if you have a number that you are good at closing, to close that number first. Optimally this would be the bull's eye, because if you are able to quickly close the "bull", you force your opponent to have to duplicate the effort or get scored on.

After closing a number always attempt to score points with that number. This not only increases your advantage but puts pressure on your opponent to close the number as quickly as possible. It also gives you some breathing room if you are scored on later in the game.

Poker Darts – Since winning each hand is worth a 100-point bonus for each player defeated, it is important to get off to a good start and put your opponent(s) at a disadvantage. Remember that the odds of getting a Royal Flush are steep, and your opponent always has the opportunity to block your intended throw if you are getting close to a winning hand. It is a safer strategy to focus on a more realistic move, such as a high-value Pair.

Once you have a Pair, you have several options: You can go for another Pair, a Full House, 3 of a Kind or 4 of a Kind. Always survey the playing field of cards before you throw and determine the cards that are clustered together and toward the center.
