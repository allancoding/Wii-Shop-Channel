How to Take a Shot
Break Shot
Adjust the placement position of the cue ball by aiming with the Pointer and pressing . Cue ball placement positions will vary depending on the contest.

Press left/right to target your first object ball. You can adjust the camera position by pressing up/down. Use the Pointer to indicate where to strike the cue ball and press .

Draw the Wii Remote back slightly while pressing , and then thrust it forward to shoot.

For a break shot to scatter object balls, you will need a strong shot. To improve your break shots, train in Practice mode.

Normal Shots
Normally, the cue ball is shot from where it lies.
If your opponent commits a foul, then you can place the cue ball anywhere you like (depending on the contest).

Deciding where to aim the cue ball is handled in the same way as a break shot. The manner in which the cue ball rolls will differ depending on where you strike. Try to control the cue ball keeping in mind the postion of your current object ball as well as the ball you would like to sink next.

A fast thrust forward with the Wii Remote allows a strong shot, while a slower thrust will result in a softer shot. In some cases, the difference can change the course of a match, so try to get a feel for the strength of your shots.
