Games & Rules

Squares (Dots)

Players take turns to try and form squares by joining dots on a grid.

 

Nine Men’s Morris (Mill)

By placing and moving counters around the grid, players try to remove each other's counters from the board. If one player reduces his opponent to 2 counters (or men), or corners his opponent so he can’t move, he is declared the winner.

 

Safe Cracker

A code-breaking logic game. The player must guess a random number within twelve attempts. The player is given hints as to how many digits and how many positions are guessed correctly.

 

Matchsticks

The player must move or remove matches to solve the puzzle.

 

Noughts And Crosses (Tic-Tac-Toe)

The aim of the game is to be the first to complete a line of either noughts or crosses. For the larger grid sizes, you score for each run of your symbol – one point for a line of three, two points for a line of four and three points for a line of five.

 

4 In-A-Row

Each player needs to create a line of four counters, in any direction, while preventing their opponent from doing the same.

 

Battlefleet

Played on a 10x10 grid, the aim is to discover your opponent’s fleet before they discover yours.

 

Hangman

The player must try to guess the mystery word before the figure on the gallows is drawn in full and is hanged. There are 7 chances for guessing the word, and each time an incorrect letter is chosen, a section of the man is drawn.
