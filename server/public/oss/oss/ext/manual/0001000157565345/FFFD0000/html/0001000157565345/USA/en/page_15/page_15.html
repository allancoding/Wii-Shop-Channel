Human Units

To build their Tower, Humans have organized themselves into six types of Units; each Unit plays a very specific role.

The Carrier

The Carrier provides the Resources required to build the Tower.

Effect of the Moods:

Happy: the Carrier handles fewer Resources and moves more slowly.

Angry: the Carrier handles more Resources and moves more quickly.

The Builder

The Builder continues the construction of the Tower using the Resources provided by the Carriers. He builds each Level's Pillars, one after the other. He also knows how to consolidate and repair Levels.

Effect of the Moods:

Happy: the Builder works and moves more slowly.

Angry: the Builder works and moves more quickly.

The Protector

The Protector moves from one floor to another with the sole aim of getting in your way. At any time, the Protector can brandish a Defensive Relic to neutralize your Powers. When this item's health points are depleted, the Protector discards it and must wait some time before being able to brandish another Defensive Relic.

Defensive Relics: four Defensive Relics are available, each related to one type of Power:

The Lightning-conducting ladle: defuses Lightning Powers.

The Sponge: absorbs Water Powers.

The Fire extinguisher: extinguishes Fire Powers.

The Magnet: suppresses Wind Powers.

When the Protector carries a Defensive Relic, he pays no attention to his Moods. If his Relic is destroyed, however, the Protector becomes angry.

Happy: the Protector moves around more slowly.

Angry: the Protector moves around more quickly.

The Construction Supervisor

The Construction Supervisor watches over the Units and drives them to work more quickly. When the Construction Supervisor stands in his slot, Builders work more quickly and Protectors do not wait to brandish a new Defensive Relic.

Effect of the Moods:

Happy: the Construction Supervisor moves around more slowly and increases the number of Respect points generated by the Units he is watching over.

Angry: the Construction Supervisor moves around more quickly and decreases the number of Respect points generated by the Units he is watching over.

The Blacksmith

The Blacksmith only enters the Tower once the Forge is built; he then settles down to build the Battering Ram, which the Humans will use to destroy the Portal leading to your realm. As soon as the Tower's Summit is built, the Blacksmith leaves the Forge with his Battering Ram, climbs to the Summit of the Tower and begins striking the Portal until it is destroyed.

To make the Blacksmith leave the Tower, you must destroy his Forge.

The Blacksmith is insensitive to Moods.

The Evil Priest

The Evil Priest enters the Tower when he believes that the Humans are too happy. He preaches evil to lower the Humans' Mood.

The Evil Priest is insensitive to Moods.

The Good Priest

He is the opposite of the Evil Priest. This Unit is entirely devoted to you. The Good Priest walks around the Tower when he believes that the Humans are too angry, preaching good and improving the Humans' Mood.

The Good Priest is insensitive to Moods.
