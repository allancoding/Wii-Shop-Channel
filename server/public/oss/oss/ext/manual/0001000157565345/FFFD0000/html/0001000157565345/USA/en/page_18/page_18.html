The Powers
General information

Three types of Powers are available:

Offensive Powers: use these to destroy the Tower's Levels.

Defensive Powers: use these to slow the Humans down or send them running, or to improve their Mood.

Combined Powers: use these to cause damage to the Tower while improving the Humans' Mood.

Specifications

Specifications common to all types of Powers

The activation level: this is the level from which a Power can be used; it is symbolized by an icon located on the Respect gauge.

The Cost: this is the cost, in sections, that you will have to pay to use a Power. It remains identical, whether you use a Power at its activation level or at a higher level.

Offensive Powers

The Power's strength level: this represents the damage (in health points) that a Power will cause to one or several Pillars.

The effect radius: defines a Power’s effect radius. The radius' size is directly related to its cost in sections.

The Power's category: Fire, Lightning, Water or Wind.

Defensive Powers

The Power's frequency: the frequency at which a Defensive Power increases or decreases Mood points.

The Power's duration: represents how long the Power remains active.

The Power's category:

  • Paralyzing: the Humans stop working.
  • Teleportation: the Humans are beamed back down to the foot of the Tower.
  • Speed: the Humans’ movement and action speed is increased or decreased.
  • Flight: the Humans are scared and run down to the Level below.

Combined Powers

These Powers have both offensive and defensive specifications.

Using Powers

Selecting a Power

Use the +Control Pad to display the Power selection interface:

Press and hold Down on the +Control Pad to display the Power selection interface.

Move the Nunchuk Control Stick in the required direction to select a Power. Then, release the +Control Pad to confirm your choice. The Power is now selected, and the selection interface disappears.

Using a Power

Point at the Level on which you want to use your Power with the Wii Remote, and press the A Button to use the selected Power.

When using Offensive Powers, move the effect radius around to strike as many Pillars as possible, while avoiding striking Humans.

When using Defensive Powers, you only need to confirm the chosen Power; it then positions itself automatically in the slot that is allocated to it.
