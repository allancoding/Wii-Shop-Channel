Hand Ranking

Order of Poker Hands

A, K, Q, J, 10 of a same suit

STRAIGHT FLUSH
Five cards of the same suit in sequence

4 OF A KIND
Four cards of the same rank

FULL HOUSE

Five cards in sequence

3 OF A KIND

TWO PAIR
Two pairs of different rank

ONE PAIR
Two cards of the same rank

HIGH CARD
Five distinct cards with one highest card



Glossary

ALL-IN: Bet all the money you have into the pot.

BET: Put money in the pot before anyone else in any given round.

BIG BLIND: The largest regular blind in a game.

BOARD: The face-up cards in a player’s hand. In Hold ‘Em, the community cards.

BUTTON: The player in the designated dealer position.

BUY-IN: The minimum amount of money required to enter any game.

CALL: Put money in the pot equal to the previous bet or raise.

CHECK: Decline to bet when it is your turn.

COMMUNITY CARDS: The cards dealt face up in the center of the table that can be used by all players to form their best hand.

FLOP: The first three community cards dealt face up at a poker table.

FOLD: Drop out of a pot rather than call a bet or raise.

LIMIT POKER: The minimum and maximum amounts a player may bet or raise in any given round of betting are fixed.

MAIN POT: A pot in which the money has been contributed by three or more players with at least one player all-in, so that there has to be a ‘side pot’ between the players who still have money. The player with no money left has a stake in the main pot only.

NO-LIMIT POKER: Players may wager any amount up to what they have in front of them in any given round.

POT-LIMIT POKER: Players may bet or raise any amount up to the current size of the pot.

RAISE: Make a bet with more chips than a previous bet.

RE-RAISE: Raise after an opponent has raised.

RIVER: The final card dealt.

SHOWDOWN: All active players’ cards are turned up at the end of the final round of betting to see who has the best hand.

SIDE POT: An additional pot for the other active players when one player is all-in.

SPLIT POT: A pot in which two or more players have hands with equal value and therefore split the money in the pot.

TURN: The fourth card dealt on the board during community card games.
