Interface Information
Signal Output Staves for violinist, violist and cellist

You need to follow the symbols that come from the right side of the Musical Staff. To achieve the correct tone you must time your strokes as the symbols pass from right to left.

The symbols may appear on the Musical Staff at different heights, lengths, and colors, which represent different notes and guide the player in performing as they would if they were using a real instrument. The upper and lower bar symbols represent up and down bow respectively; the longer the bar the longer your bow movement and vice versa.

To play successfully, you also need to pay attention to the color of the symbols and make sure that your bow is the same color when you stroke to play those symbols.

To make your bow color blue, press and hold C button on the Nunchuk. If a Nunchuk is not available, you can still get the color blue by pressing and holding A button on the Wii Remote.

To make your bow color red, press and hold Z button on the Nunchuk or B button on the Wii Remote.

To make your bow color green, press and hold C+Z buttons on the Nunchuk or A+B buttons on the Wii Remote.

Signal Output Arc for conductor

To ensure a stable tempo, the conductor needs to control the baton and have it stay in the safe zone, which is the central part of the arc. If it falls outside of the safe zone the rhythm will be off-tempo.

In order to keep the baton in the safe zone you must wave the Wii Remote to hit notes dropped from the top of the signal output arc.

Invincible State Gauge

Every successful play made by the three instrument players will be accumulated and turned into a Star, which can be activated by the player as an Invincibility power-up. During this period any mistakes made are not registered. Each Star can activate the power for five seconds.

Whenever instant scoring or relief is needed, the conductor can press and hold A+B buttons on Wii Remote to activate the Invincibility power-up. It can be used up to three times in accordance with the number of Stars accumulated. While activated instrument players can move the Wii Remote freely.

HP Indicator

The HP indicator tells you how many Health Points the other instrument players currently have. If you fail to hit the notes in time and perform poorly their HP will decrease until the game ends. If you hit the notes in time and play successfully it can help increase their HP, however the conductor's HP will not recover.

Background Objects

The game environment dynamically changes and can be enriched with more objects if you play successfully. Check the background and see if it is changed by your play.
