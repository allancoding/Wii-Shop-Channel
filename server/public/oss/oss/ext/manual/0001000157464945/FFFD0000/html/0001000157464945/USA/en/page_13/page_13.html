Fighting

Once you hook a fish, it's time for the fight! There are 3 actions fish take during a fight:

 

Resting : When the fish is resting, it swims and moves at a slow, normal pace. This is your chance to bring it in close, so reel as fast as you can! Make sure you hold the Wii Remote flat when you reel, though. If you hold the Wii Remote up while you reel, it will put extra stress on your line, and eventually cause it to break!

Struggling: When the fish starts struggling, you'll feel a pulsing Rumble in the Wii Remote, and the fish will violently shake its body back and forth. When this happens, stop reeling and hold the Wii Remote upright. Whatever you do, donâ€™t reel while you have the Wii Remote upright. The sudden tension will cause the line to break. Also, be sure not to lay the Wii Remote flat, either--the line will go slack, and the hook will come out.


Fleeing: When a fish flees, you'll feel a short, sustained Rumble in the Wii Remote, and the fish will start rapidly swimming away from you. When this happens, lay the Wii Remote flat and reel quickly. This will keep the fish from putting too much stress on the line and causing it to break. Make sure you don't hold the Wii Remote up while you're reeling, though, or the added stress will break the line.
