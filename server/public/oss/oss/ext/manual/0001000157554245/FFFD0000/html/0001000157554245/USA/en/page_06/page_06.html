GAME MECHANICS

Preparation


The jewels are placed randomly on the board. Every player has a pawn and places it on any of the fields on the game board. There may be several pawns on the same field.


The solving of the puzzle

 

Every player has one plate and a time limit. They look at their own plates. The task is to (as quickly as possible) exactly fill the squared area on the plate using the tiles. The other players continue working on their task as long as time keeps running.

 

The moving of the pawn and seizing of the jewels

 

The first player to solve his puzzle may move his pawn up to three steps in any direction on the red fields. The player then picks up the two closest jewels in the row the pawn is standing in. The second player to solve his puzzle may move his pawn up to two steps, and then seize the two closest jewels in the pawn’s row. Should a third player manage to solve his puzzle before time is up, he too may move his pawn, but only up to one step. He then seizes the two closest jewels in the pawns row. And finally, if the fourth player solves his puzzle, he may not move his pawn, but nevertheless seize two jewels in the same manner as everyone else.

Every player that solves his puzzle in time may take two jewels. The only advantage a faster player has is that he may move his pawn further. This makes sense, because the total number of collected jewels is irrelevant; it is the number of jewels from one color that counts. The jewels in possession of every player are always public.

 

The end of a round

 

When time is up, the round ends. Every player receives a new plate in order to solve a new puzzle. A new round begins.

 

The end of the game

 

The player with the most jewels in a single color wins the game.
