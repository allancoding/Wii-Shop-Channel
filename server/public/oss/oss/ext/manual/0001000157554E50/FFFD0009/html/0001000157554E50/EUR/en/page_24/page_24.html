Going Out
When a player plays his or her second-to-last card, he or she must call out “UNO™” (meaning “ONE”). This warns all players that he or she can go out on the next turn. If a player fails to say “UNO™” by the time his or her card is discarded, any other player in the game may “catch” that player by selecting the icon that appears. If a player is “caught,” he or she immediately draws two cards from the draw pile, and play continues. A player who does not say “UNO™” must be caught before the next player plays or else he or she is “safe,” and there is no penalty.



When a player plays his or her last card, the hand is over. Points are scored, the cards are collected and reshuffled and a new game is started. If the last card played is a Draw Two or Wild Draw Four card, the next player must draw two or four cards and these cards are counted when the points are totalled.

If no one goes out by the time the draw pile is depleted, the discard pile is reshuffled, except the top card, and play continues with the reshuffled draw pile.
